What is PF Withdrawal?

PF withdrawal is the process of claiming the accumulated balance in your Employees’ Provident Fund account when you become eligible, such as after leaving a job or under specific conditions.

It can help startups access tax benefits, easier compliance, networking opportunities and better visibility in government and corporate programs.

Who is this service for?

  • New companies and LLPs with innovative products or services.
  • Early-stage founders planning to raise funding.
  • Startups working in technology or unique business models.
  • Existing small businesses that recently started operations.

Key Benefits

  • Official Startup recognition certificate.
  • Access to selected tax and compliance benefits (as per rules).
  • Better image in front of investors and partners.
  • Easier participation in Startup programmes and events.
  • Increased credibility for your business.

Documents Required

  • Incorporation certificate of the company/LLP.
  • PAN of the entity and founders.
  • Brief pitch or write-up about the business and innovation.
  • Website or product details (if any).
  • Other supporting documents as required by the portal.

Quick FAQs

Can I withdraw PF while still working?

Full withdrawal is normally allowed after leaving service. Partial withdrawals are possible in special situations, as per EPFO rules.

How long does PF withdrawal usually take?

After correct filing, it generally takes a few working weeks, depending on EPFO processing.

Is PAN mandatory for PF withdrawal?

Most of the process can be completed online through documents and calls.PAN is strongly recommended. Non-availability may lead to higher TDS deduction in some cases.
